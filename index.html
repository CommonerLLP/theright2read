Not neglect. A pattern.

Promised as a commons. Delivered as a deferral.

For seventy-five years the public library has been avowed and deferred in the same breath — committees that costed it precisely, then never built it — until deferral set into refusal.

1948
India's first Library Act charges readers a fee — breaking the "free" law on the day it passes.
1986
The Chattopadhyay Committee says public libraries must be free and State-funded. The report is received, then shelved.
1993
The Centre reconsiders the 1986 recommendations — and shelves them a second time. The library question goes silent for a decade.
2016
A cabinet reclassification quietly files the National Mission on Libraries into an "optional" bucket no state is obliged to fund.
2020
The Mission is declared "complete" — three months after launching a single website. The library budget that year: ₹0.73 crore spent of ₹118.51 crore allocated.

In 2017 China gave its people a Public Library Law. India still has none.
