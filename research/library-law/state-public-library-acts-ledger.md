**Standing rule (do not violate):** secondary LIS literature routinely conflates
*"has an Act"* with *"has a cess."* A row is only `PRIMARY` once the **statute text
itself** has been read. Until then it is `SECONDARY` (from studies/committee
reports) or `PENDING`. See auto-memory `feedback-verify-acts-before-classifying`.

**Source flags:** `PRIMARY` = Act text read · `SECONDARY` = from study/report ·
`PENDING` = not yet located/read.

## Landscape facts (verified this turn)

- **~half of Indian states/UTs have *no* Public Libraries Act.** — RTR convergence
  paper, *Public Libraries and Indian Democracy 2014–2025* (2026), Zotero D2GV9CAH. SECONDARY.
- **First Library Act in India:** Kolhapur Princely State, **1945** (British rule). SECONDARY.
- **First post-independence state Act:** Madras (later Tamil Nadu) Public Libraries
  Act, **24 March 1948**. SECONDARY (multiple).
- **Five states with a statutory library cess:** **Tamil Nadu, Andhra Pradesh,
  Karnataka, West Bengal, Kerala.** Only **Tamil Nadu's** cess produced durably-rising
  real per-capita spending; the other four are "failures of operation," not
  legislation. — convergence paper §3.4–3.5, D2GV9CAH. SECONDARY.
- **National Delivery Act:** Delivery of Books and Newspapers (Public Libraries)
  Act, **1954** — legal-deposit; compliance weak. SECONDARY.
