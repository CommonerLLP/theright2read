## ⚠ HEADLINE CORRECTION — the cess states (primary statute, 2026-06-27)

The convergence paper (D2GV9CAH / 4RLIRDP3, SECONDARY) says five cess states:
**TN, AP, Karnataka, West Bengal, Kerala.** Reading the Act texts **overturns this**:

- **West Bengal has NO library cess** (Act 1979) — its Library Fund is endowments +
  government grants + amounts collected under rules; it lacks the "'library cess'
  means the cess levied…" definition every cess Act carries.
- **Haryana (1989) and Goa (1993) DO have a library cess** — both omitted from the list.
- **Corrected cess set (7): Tamil Nadu, Andhra Pradesh, Telangana, Karnataka, Kerala,
  Haryana, Goa** — NOT West Bengal. (Telangana = the AP 1960 Act adapted, §20 cess.)
- **Cess rates — DEFINITIVE (IIHS Working Paper 2018, Appendix 2; cross-checked w/ Acts):**
  TN **10%** (revised up: 3 paise 1950 → 5 paise 1972 → **10 paise 1992**) · AP **8%**
  (4 paise base) · Telangana = AP regime · Karnataka **6%** (3 paise + 6% land-revenue
  grant; Rules 1966) · Kerala **5%** · Goa **1.67%** (surcharge on excise/liquor).
  Haryana: cess **provision Yes but no operative rate** ("−"); WB: **No cess**.
- **Cess count reconciled across 3 authoritative sources + statute:** *provision* = 7
  (TN/AP/Telangana/KA/Kerala/Haryana/Goa) · *operative with a rate* = 5–6 (TN/AP/KA/
  Kerala/Goa [+Telangana]). IIHS 2018 headline "5", IFLA 2025 "6 southern", my statute
  reads "7 provisions" — all reconcile once provision-vs-operative is separated. WB never.
- **Gazette/DPL layer (Mumbai-box pass, 2026-06-27):** Karnataka DPL = full Act+Rules+
  notifications (done). Reachable for a deeper crawl: tn.gov.in, kslc.kerala.gov.in,
  dol.goa.gov.in (Acts&Rules), highereduhry (Haryana). **Down/blocked even from an Indian
  IP:** publiclibraries.ap.gov.in (403), Telangana libraries dept, TN public-library org,
  and the state e-gazette portals (egazette.nic.in / kerala / tn) — so the per-state Rules
  layer is only partly obtainable; many state portals are simply offline.
- Cess **base varies**: property-tax surcharge (TN, AP, Karnataka, Kerala, Haryana)
  vs **liquor** (Goa). Only TN's cess durably raised real per-capita spend (convergence §3.5).
- **ACTION before the convergence paper / WP-002 ships:** drop WB, add Haryana + Goa,
  note Goa's liquor base. (Claim is NOT in repo `.qmd` source — it lives in the
  convergence paper PDF; fix at that paper's source.)

## Authoritative corroboration + currency (Balaji et al / IFLA 2025; PNLP; Vidhi)

**The 19-state count is now QUADRUPLE-confirmed:** our primary statute reads + IIHS 2018
+ IFLA 2025 + the PNLP. Sources:
- **B. Preedip Balaji, M.S. Vinay & J.S. Mohan Raju, "A Policy Review of Public Libraries
  in India," IIHS Working Paper 2018|01** (March 2018; DOI 10.24943/prpliwp1.2018; 92pp;
  on iCloud, NOT in Zotero). "19 states have passed legislation, of which only 5 have a
  library cess." **Appendix 2 = per-state salient-features table** (Act/year · cess Y/N ·
  rate% · fund composition) — the definitive per-state survey; **Appendix 4 = state-wise
  public-library data**; Appendix 3 = the RTI on per-capita spend (MoC: "no official data").
- **Kulkarni, Balaji & Dhanamjaya, "Toward Reforms: Revisiting Public Library Legislation
  in Indian States," IFLA Journal 2025** (Zotero GIMCMTH7/442J9WDK): "**19 states and UTs
  have library laws**"; "**only six southern states currently levy a library tax, 13
  [legislated] states do not mandate one, and 17 states/UTs lack any library legislation**."
  Karnataka cess **6 paise/rupee** max (confirms our DPL read). **CAG audit:** Karnataka
  failed to amend the KPLA / monitor cess remittance → non-compliance; cess collections
  declined. Kerala & Goa allocate ~1% of education budgets.
- **FLN People's National Library Policy 2024** (on disk): "**19 Indian states have public
  library legislation, although only 1 state (Haryana) defines the public library as free
  from subscription fees**."
- **Vidhi Centre for Legal Policy, "Putting the Public Back in Public Libraries:
  Restructuring the Governance of Public Libraries in Maharashtra," Feb 2022** (m1:
  `fiddlewiddle/public-finance/data/Vidhi_MH_Public_Libraries.pdf`; orphaned PDF in
  rights2reads): Maharashtra funding inadequate, no cess; recommends governance restructuring.

**Cess — provision vs practice (reconciled):**
- *Statutory cess provision (our primary reads): ~7* — TN, AP, Telangana, Karnataka, Kerala,
  Haryana, Goa.
- *Actually levied (Balaji et al): ~6* — the 5 southern + **Puducherry** (adopts the TN Act).
  **Haryana's 1989 cess and Goa's liquor cess appear on-paper / not operatively levied** —
  Balaji counts them among the "13 legislated states that do not mandate" a tax in practice.
- **17 states/UTs have NO library legislation** (of 28 states + 8 UTs).

## Landscape

- **~half of states/UTs have no Public Libraries Act** (convergence paper). SECONDARY.

## The Gujarat case — 1977 Bill → 2001 Act (24-year deferral)

From the **1978 Select Committee report** on Bill No. 56 of 1977 (Zotero 5SCF7ZU7/RIYYM3ST):
the bill was drafted, debated, and **committee-approved by 1978** ("the Committee recommends
that the Bill as amended be passed," cl. 29). The gap was **finance + will, not drafting**:
- **No library cess.** Funding = a State Library Fund drawn from the **Consolidated Fund of
  the State** (annual appropriation) → library competes in the general budget yearly, no
  ring-fenced revenue.
- Even that contested: the **Rs 50-lakh initial fund called insufficient**; making local
  authorities contribute = "additional burden" (cls. 27–28).
- → No dedicated money, no constituency forcing it through → lapsed across assembly cycles
  for ~23 years → finally enacted **2001 (Guj. Act 25 of 2001)** — still **with no cess**
  (State Library Development Fund). The national deferral in miniature: costed/approved
  1977–78, shelved a generation for want of a funding mechanism, enacted toothless in 2001.

**Political context (verified timeline; explains the 24-year stall).** The bill (1977) +
Select Cttee (1978) sit inside **Babubhai J. Patel's second Janata govt (11 Apr 1977–17 Feb
1980), which fell into President's Rule in Feb 1980.** The decade was convulsions. **The tinder (1972–73):**
drought in both 1972 & 1973 → food scarcity; the Oct-1973 **oil shock** quadrupled oil
prices → inflation + cooking-oil/food shortages; Congress installed **Chimanbhai Patel**
(17 Jul 1973, via defections), promptly "**Chiman chor**" amid the groundnut/cooking-oil
corruption racket. **The spark:** a 20% hostel mess-fee hike at LD College of Engineering,
Ahmedabad (20 Dec 1973) → the **Navnirman Andolan 1973–74**, a middle-class anti-corruption /
anti-price-rise revolt that toppled Chimanbhai (resigned 9 Feb 1974) → President's Rule. Then
June 1975 Janata Morcha + the Emergency; March 1976 President's Rule; Dec 1976 Solanki; Apr
1977 Babubhai again; Feb 1980 President's Rule — **~4 govt changes + 2–3 President's-Rule
spells, 1974–80.** Then **Solanki's KHAM politics → 1981 & 1985 anti-reservation riots** consumed the
polity. A low-priority no-cess library bill had no chance amid collapsing governments and
caste-reservation war; enacted only under the stabilised post-1995 BJP regime, in 2001 — and
still toothless. **Campaign point:** the reading commons sat deferred *precisely while caste
tore the state apart.* (Timeline verified: Navnirman Andolan; Gujarat CM list,
gujaratindia.gov.in. Causal link = grounded interpretation, not a single documented source.)

**Genesis (1977) — the RRRLF/central climate.** The template is a **centrally-circulated model library bill** from the early-1960s
Nehruvian state — referenced in the **ILA 1964 proceedings** as a Model Bill "under the active
consideration of the State Government" (and in WP-008). ⚠ **CAVEAT (RULE ZERO, 2026-06-28):**
we do **NOT hold the bill text**, and the precise **"1963" dating is UNVERIFIED** from a held
primary source. What we physically hold is (a) an **undated** "Model Union Library Act"
(union/national libraries — a *different* model), (b) the Compendium's "Model Public Libraries"
as a *grant head* (not a bill), (c) Venkatappaiah's *Model Library Legislation* (1994,
secondary). **To verify before claiming "1963 / Nehru's penultimate year": find the actual
model-bill document + its date.** Post-Nehru, **RRRLF** (Raja Rammohun Roy Library Foundation,
est. **1972**, MoC; Indira era) added **matching grants**; together the central model-bill effort
+ RRRLF seeded the mid-1970s state-bill wave — **Gujarat AND Kerala both introduced bills in 1977** (the contemporaneity is
the central prompt's fingerprint). But the push was an **incentive, not a mandate** — IIHS 2018:
the Centre "hesitates to insist" on legislation — so a no-cess bill could still be ignored.
The same softness that seeded it is why it stalled. (The Gujarat bill's own 1978 record names
only generic "Central/State Government" grants, not RRRLF directly — RRRLF = climate, not a
documented genesis.)

**Enactment (2001) — Keshubhai Patel, not Modi.** Assented by the Governor **1 Sept 2001**
(Guj. Act 25 of 2001). CM was **Keshubhai Patel (BJP, Mar 1998–6 Oct 2001)**; **Modi became CM
7 Oct 2001** — so the Act passed **~5 weeks before Modi**, a Keshubhai-era enactment. Likely
impetus: a stable BJP majority clearing the long-pending bill (legislative housekeeping the
unstable 1977–95 governments couldn't manage) — but enacted **still with no cess** (statutory
tick-box, not funded commitment). The gazette carries no Statement of Objects; the specific
driver would need the Gujarat Assembly debates (not in corpus). NML climate (2012–14) post-dates it.
